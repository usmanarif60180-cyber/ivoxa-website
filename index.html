How to Write an Invoice That Gets Paid Faster

The details that make clients pay on time — from clear payment terms to the exact information an invoice needs to avoid delays.

TI
The IVOXA Team
May 04, 2026 · 4 min read
SHARE

Late payments are rarely about a client refusing to pay — they're usually about friction. A confusing invoice, a missing due date, or an unclear payment method can push your invoice to the bottom of someone's inbox.

Put the essentials where they can't be missed

  • A clear invoice number, so both sides can reference it in emails
  • The exact amount due, in a large, unambiguous font
  • A due date — not just "payment terms: Net 30" buried in fine print
  • Your payment details right on the document

Set payment terms before the work starts

The best time to agree on payment terms is before you start the project, not when the invoice lands. State the due date, any late fees, and accepted payment methods in your quote or contract.

💡 Shorter terms get paid faster. "Due on receipt" or "Net 7" consistently outperforms "Net 30" for small invoices.

Make the invoice itself easy to trust

Include your business name, address and tax/VAT number, and keep the design clean and consistent every time. Clients pay faster when an invoice looks like it came from an established business.

Free Invoice Generator for Germany

German invoices (Rechnungen) have specific mandatory fields under the UStG. Missing one is the most common reason a German client's accounting team rejects an invoice.

Create a Germany invoice — free View invoice template
Currency
EUR (€)
Consumption tax
MwSt / USt (VAT)

Business requirements

  • A Steuernummer (tax number) or USt-IdNr (VAT ID) depending on registration
  • Confirmation of whether you qualify for the Kleinunternehmer VAT exemption
  • A registered business address

What a compliant invoice needs

  • A unique, sequential invoice number
  • Full name and address of both parties
  • Net amount, VAT rate, VAT amount and gross total shown separately
⚠️ General overview, not tax advice. Confirm current MwSt rates with a Steuerberater or the Bundeszentralamt für Steuern.
